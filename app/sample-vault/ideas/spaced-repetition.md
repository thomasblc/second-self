# Spaced repetition

Memory is a leaky bucket and review is the refill. The trick is to review just before you would have forgotten, so each repetition pushes the next one further out. Cram ten times in an hour and you remember it for a day. Review five times across a month and you remember it for years.

I want my [[projects/second-self-app]] to eventually surface old notes at the right interval, not just on search. A second brain that only answers when asked is a filing cabinet. One that resurfaces a half-finished idea on the morning you need it is a collaborator.

Pairs naturally with [[ideas/atomic-notes]]: you can only schedule review of something small enough to review.

A rough cadence that has worked for me:

| Review | Gap |
|---|---|
| 1st | next day |
| 2nd | 3 days |
| 3rd | 1 week |
| 4th | 1 month |
