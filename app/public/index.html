Models

Pick the model your second self runs on. Everything downloads once into ~/.qvac/models, is shared across the app, and stays on your machine. Open models link to their weights on Hugging Face.

Fine-tunable bases can learn your voice (train a LoRA on your notes). Chat-only models are great to talk to but cannot be trained in this SDK. The recommended base to train on is Qwen3 1.7B (proven), or BitNet b1.58 3B for the largest fine-tunable model.

Your vaultsA vault is a folder of markdown notes on your disk. Switch between them, or open another folder.
Open a folder as a vaultPoint Second Self at any folder of notes you already have.
Create a new vaultMake a fresh folder with a starter note and switch to it.
Import ChatGPT / Claude chatsTurn your exported conversations into notes you can search and train on.
Index vault for memoryEmbeds your notes on-device so the chat's Memory toggle can retrieve real facts from them (RAG). Re-run after adding a lot of notes.
Vault syncYour notes are plain .md files on disk. If your vault folder lives in iCloud, Dropbox, or a git repo, those tools sync it across devices, and Second Self picks up external edits automatically (no save button needed). There is no Second Self cloud: nothing is uploaded.
Make this a master machineThis machine keeps the vault and runs the model. Other devices connect to it and work on this vault over an encrypted peer-to-peer link (works across networks, nothing goes through a server). It gives you a pairing code to share.
Connect to a master machineMake this device a thin client: paste a master's pairing code to browse its vault and run inference on it. Nothing is stored here.
Share just this machine's GPUAdvanced: let another device borrow this machine's GPU for inference while its own files stay on it (delegated inference).
Borrow a remote GPUAdvanced: run chat on another machine's GPU; your vault stays here.
Second SelfA local, private notes vault that learns to talk like you. Your notes, your model, your machine. Nothing is ever uploaded.
